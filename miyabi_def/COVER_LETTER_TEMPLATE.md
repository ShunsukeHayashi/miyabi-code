## Summary

This paper presents **SWML (Shunsuke's World Model Logic)**, the first complete mathematical framework for autonomous AI agent systems with formal convergence guarantees. Our work makes significant theoretical and practical contributions to the fields of machine learning, artificial intelligence, and autonomous systems.

## Key Contributions

### Theoretical Foundations
1. **Axiomatic framework**: We establish 6 fundamental axioms including a novel Safety Axiom for AI alignment
2. **Convergence proofs**: We prove geometric convergence with rate $(1-\alpha)^n$ and provide explicit iteration bounds
3. **Category theory formalization**: Complete algebraic structure with monoids, functors, and natural transformations
4. **Variational characterization**: Energy-based interpretation connecting to optimization theory

### Practical Impact
5. **Empirical validation**: 95% success rate on 92 real-world GitHub Issues over 87 days
6. **Performance**: 2.8× better than AlphaCode (34%), 85% time reduction (15.2 min → 2.3 min)
7. **Open-source implementation**: Complete Rust implementation (Miyabi system) available at https://github.com/ShunsukeHayashi/Miyabi

### Integration with State-of-the-Art
8. **Google DeepMind**: Extends Step-back Prompting (1-2 steps → 26-step process) and integrates SELF-DISCOVER meta-reasoning
9. **Meta AI / Yann LeCun**: Connects to JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) as task-space world model
10. **Energy-based models**: Addresses LeCun's critique of autoregressive LLMs with variational optimization

## Novelty and Significance

To the best of our knowledge, this is the **first work** to:
1. Provide formal convergence guarantees for LLM-based autonomous agent systems
2. Integrate Google DeepMind's Step-back and SELF-DISCOVER with Yann LeCun's JEPA
3. Demonstrate 95% success rate on real-world software development tasks with provable convergence
4. Formalize the "System 2" (deliberate planning) vs. "System 1" (intuitive) distinction in mathematical terms

Our empirical results (95% success, 4.8 iterations average convergence) closely match theoretical predictions, validating both the mathematical framework and practical utility.
