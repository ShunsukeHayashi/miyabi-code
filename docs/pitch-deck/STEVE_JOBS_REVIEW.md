## What Steve Would Change Immediately

### 1. CUT SLIDES IN HALF

**Current**: 30+ slides
**Target**: 12 slides

**Rule**: If a slide doesn't make them gasp, cut it.

---

### 2. ADD EMOTION

Your deck is too rational. It's all numbers and logic.

**Where's the emotion?**
- Show me a frustrated CIO drowning in 10 AI tools
- Show me the relief when everything just works
- Show me the DEMO (video or live)

**Remember**: People don't buy with logic. They buy with emotion and justify with logic.

---

### 3. SHOW, DON'T TELL

**Current**: "Miyabi processes 47 emails"
**Better**: **[SCREENSHOT of inbox going from 47 ‚Üí 0]**

**Current**: "We have 3 enterprise pilots"
**Better**: **[LOGOS of the 3 companies]**

**Current**: "Singapore government signed $10M"
**Better**: **[PHOTO of signed contract or partnership announcement]**

**Visuals beat words. Always.**

---

### 4. FIX THE DEMO

You have ONE slide about the core product experience:

```
"Miyabi, what's my day?"
‚úÖ 47 emails processed
‚úÖ 3 meetings scheduled
```

This should be **3-5 slides** or **a 2-minute video**.

**Why?** Because this is what they're buying. Show them magic.

**Demo Script**:
1. Screen 1: Inbox with 47 unread emails
2. Screen 2: "Miyabi, handle my emails"
3. Screen 3: Miyabi processing (real-time)
4. Screen 4: Inbox at 0, responses sent, meetings scheduled
5. Screen 5: "All done. Here's your day."

**Total time**: 2 minutes
**Impact**: They'll remember this forever

---

### 5. KILL THE APPENDIX

You have 5 appendix slides. **Delete them all.**

**Why?** If it's in the appendix, it doesn't matter.

If someone asks a question, answer it verbally or have backup slides ready. But don't put them in the deck.

**The appendix is where good presentations go to die.**
