### Detailed Community Rules

#### 1. Channel-Appropriate Content ‚úÖ
**Post in the Right Place**
- Read channel descriptions and use channels for their intended purpose
- Use threads for extended discussions in busy channels
- Move conversations to appropriate channels when topics shift
- Check pinned messages for channel-specific guidelines

**Channel Guide**:
- **#help-**: Specific technical questions with context and details
- **#development-**: Code discussions, architecture, and design topics
- **#showcase-**: Completed projects and demonstrations
- **#general-chat**: Community discussions related to AI agents
- **#enterprise-**: Business use cases and commercial discussions

#### 2. Quality Technical Content üîß
**Provide Context and Details**
- Include relevant environment details (OS, versions, configurations)
- Share error messages and logs when reporting issues
- Provide minimal reproducible examples for bugs
- Explain what you've already tried before asking for help

**Code Sharing Best Practices**:
- Use proper code formatting (triple backticks with language)
- Include relevant imports and dependencies
- Explain the purpose and expected behavior
- Share working examples rather than pseudocode

#### 3. Respectful Professional Discourse üí¨
**Maintain Professional Standards**
- Treat all community members with respect and dignity
- Welcome newcomers and help them get oriented
- Provide constructive feedback and criticism
- Acknowledge when you don't know something

**Conflict Resolution**:
- Disagree with ideas, not people
- Assume good intentions in communications
- Focus on technical merits of solutions
- Escalate to moderators if discussions become heated

#### 4. No Spam or Low-Effort Content üö´
**Contribute Meaningful Content**
- Avoid excessive self-promotion (follow 9:1 rule - 9 helpful contributions for every 1 promotional post)
- No duplicate posts across multiple channels
- Search existing discussions before posting common questions
- Avoid one-word or emoji-only responses in serious technical discussions

**Self-Promotion Guidelines**:
- Clearly label promotional content
- Focus on educational value for the community
- Engage in discussion beyond just promoting your content
- Respect community feedback about promotional frequency

#### 5. Privacy and Security Awareness üîí
**Protect Sensitive Information**
- Never share API keys, passwords, or authentication tokens
- Use dummy data in code examples and screenshots
- Respect NDAs and confidential business information
- Be cautious about sharing production environment details

**Security Best Practices**:
- Sanitize logs and error messages before sharing
- Use environment variables for sensitive configuration
- Follow security guidelines when sharing deployment information
- Report security vulnerabilities through proper channels

#### 6. Attribution and Intellectual Property üìÑ
**Respect Others' Work**
- Credit sources when sharing external content
- Respect open source licenses and terms
- Ask permission before sharing others' private work
- Follow fair use guidelines for copyrighted material

**Content Sharing Guidelines**:
- Link to original sources when discussing external content
- Quote appropriately rather than copying entire articles
- Acknowledge contributors and collaborators
- Respect proprietary information and trade secrets

### Community Violations & Consequences

#### Minor Violations (Friendly Reminder)
**Examples**:
- Posting in wrong channel
- Insufficient context in help requests
- Minor off-topic discussions
- Formatting issues with code

**Response**: Helpful redirection with explanation

#### Moderate Violations (Official Warning)
**Examples**:
- Repeated posting in wrong channels after guidance
- Low-effort spam or excessive self-promotion
- Dismissive or unhelpful responses to genuine questions
- Persistent off-topic discussions

**Response**: Public warning, temporary channel restrictions

#### Serious Violations (Temporary Ban)
**Examples**:
- Disrespectful or hostile behavior toward community members
- Sharing inappropriate or offensive content
- Deliberately misleading technical advice
- Violation of privacy or security guidelines

**Response**: 7-day temporary ban, required acknowledgment before return

#### Severe Violations (Permanent Ban)
**Examples**:
- Harassment, discrimination, or hate speech
- Malicious code sharing or security violations
- Coordinated disruption or raid attempts
- Doxxing or sharing private information

**Response**: Immediate permanent ban, no appeals process

### Moderation Philosophy & Approach

#### Our Moderation Principles

**1. Community-Driven Standards**
- Rules emerge from community values and needs
- Members help enforce standards through positive peer pressure
- Moderation supports rather than replaces community self-governance
- Transparency in moderation decisions and processes

**2. Educational Over Punitive**
- First response focuses on education and guidance
- Help members understand community expectations
- Provide clear paths for improvement and redemption
- Escalate only when educational approaches fail

**3. Context-Aware Decisions**
- Consider intent, context, and community impact
- Account for cultural and language differences
- Recognize that technical discussions can become heated
- Balance individual needs with community health

**4. Consistent and Fair Application**
- Apply rules consistently regardless of member status
- Document decisions for transparency and learning
- Regular review of moderation practices and outcomes
- Community feedback on moderation effectiveness

#### Moderation Escalation Process

**Level 1: Community Self-Moderation**
- Community members provide gentle corrections
- Peer education and positive reinforcement
- Natural consequences of community response
- Moderators observe but don't intervene

**Level 2: Moderator Guidance**
- Private message with explanation and guidance
- Public clarification of community expectations
- Temporary channel restrictions if needed
- Documentation of interaction for future reference

**Level 3: Formal Warning**
- Official warning with clear explanation of violation
- Specific expectations for behavior change
- Temporary restrictions on posting or channel access
- Required acknowledgment and agreement to comply

**Level 4: Temporary Suspension**
- 7-day suspension from community participation
- Clear explanation of violation and expectations
- Required discussion with moderator before reinstatement
- Probationary period upon return

**Level 5: Permanent Removal**
- Permanent ban for severe violations or repeated offenses
- Appeal process through admin team for certain cases
- Public notification for transparency (without personal details)
- Coordination with related communities if necessary

### Appeals and Dispute Resolution

#### Appeal Process for Moderation Actions

**Step 1: Internal Review**
- Contact the moderator who took the action
- Provide clear explanation of your perspective
- Request reconsideration with additional context
- Timeline: Response within 24 hours

**Step 2: Moderator Team Review**
- If unsatisfied with initial response, request team review
- All available moderators review the case
- Consider additional evidence and context
- Timeline: Response within 48 hours

**Step 3: Administrative Review**
- Final appeal to administrative team
- Independent review of all evidence and decisions
- Consider precedent and community impact
- Timeline: Response within 72 hours

**Step 4: Community Input** (for significant cases)
- For cases affecting community direction or precedent
- Anonymous community feedback collection
- Transparent discussion of principles and decisions
- Final decision with explanation to community

### Special Guidelines by Channel Category

**Enhanced Guidelines for Help Channels**

**Asking for Help Effectively**:

### Frequently Asked Questions

#### Community Guidelines FAQ

**Q: Can I share my commercial projects built with Miyabi?**
A: Yes! We encourage sharing projects that demonstrate real-world value. Focus on the technical implementation, challenges solved, and lessons learned rather than pure marketing.

**Q: What's the difference between #help-general and other help channels?**
A: Use #help-general for broad questions or when you're unsure which specific channel fits. Use specialized channels (#help-github-app, #help-agents, etc.) when you know the specific area of your question.

**Q: How do I get recognized as a contributor or expert?**
A: Consistently provide high-quality help, contribute to open source projects, create valuable content, and demonstrate deep expertise over time. Recognition roles are earned through community contribution.

**Q: Can I promote my company's services or products?**
A: Limited promotion is allowed if it provides value to the community. Follow the 9:1 rule: make 9 helpful contributions for every 1 promotional post. Focus on educational value.

**Q: What should I do if I see behavior that violates community guidelines?**
A: Use the report functionality to alert moderators, or mention @Moderator in the channel. Provide context about the specific violation you observed.

**Q: How can I suggest improvements to the community?**
A: Use #feedback-suggestions to propose improvements. Be specific about the problem you want to solve and your proposed solution.

**Q: What if I accidentally violate a guideline?**
A: Acknowledge the mistake, learn from the guidance provided, and adjust your behavior. Most violations are learning opportunities rather than serious offenses.

**Q: How do I verify my professional or enterprise status?**
A: Contact moderators with verification of your subscription or enterprise contract. This gives access to professional and enterprise channels.

## For Moderators: Enforcement Guidelines

### Moderation Decision Framework

#### Assessment Criteria for Violations

**Intent Assessment**:
- Was the violation intentional or accidental?
- Is this a pattern of behavior or isolated incident?
- Did the member respond positively to previous guidance?
- What was the impact on community atmosphere?

**Severity Evaluation**:
- Direct harm to individuals or community
- Disruption to community function
- Violation of trust or safety
- Legal or ethical implications

**Context Considerations**:
- Member's history and contributions
- Cultural or language barriers
- Technical vs. interpersonal violations
- Community standards evolution

#### Moderation Response Templates

**Gentle Redirection**:
```
Hi @username! This kind of discussion would be better suited for #channel-name. Could you repost there? Thanks for understanding!
```

**Rule Clarification**:
```
@username, just a reminder that we ask for [specific guideline]. This helps keep our community focused and helpful for everyone. Here's a link to our full guidelines: [link]
```

**Official Warning**:
```
@username, this is an official warning for [specific violation]. Please review our community guidelines [link] and adjust your participation accordingly. Continued violations may result in restrictions.
```

**Escalation Notice**:
```
@username, due to repeated violations of our community guidelines, you're being placed on a temporary restriction. Please review our guidelines and contact a moderator when you're ready to discuss returning to full participation.
```

### Moderator Decision Log

**Required Documentation for All Actions**:
- Date, time, and moderator taking action
- Specific violation and evidence
- Assessment of intent and severity
- Action taken and reasoning
- Member response and follow-up needed

**Monthly Review Process**:
- Review all moderation actions for consistency
- Identify patterns requiring guideline updates
- Assess effectiveness of educational approaches
- Gather community feedback on moderation quality
